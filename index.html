Intro

Welcome to Friendpurr! With FriendPurr, you can quickly learn to identify some of the most common accessibility errors. Let’s get started!

Instructions

  1. Turn on the screen reader:
    • On a Chromebook, turn on ChromeVox by pressing Ctrl + Alt + Z. Remember that you can press Ctrl to silence speech at any time. If you haven’t used ChromeVox, consider running through our tutorial by pressing Search + O, then T.
    • On Mac, you can triple press the power button on the touch bar to turn on and use ctrl + option and the arrow keys to move and ctrl + option space to select.
    • NVDA on Windows: Download the free and open source screen reader here Enable: Ctrl + Alt + N Disable: Insert + Q
  2. Navigate through the page using the keyboard and screen reader:
    • ChromeVox on Chrome OS Use ChromeVox navigation (Search + Right arrow to go forwards, or Search + Left arrow to go backwards) to navigate through the page and explore its accessibility.
    • Voiceover on Mac
      • Press Ctrl + Option + Right to move forward item by item, Ctrl + Option + Left to move backwards
      • When you want to move to a different part of the browser (e.g. from the toolbar to the web content) you will be prompted to enter that section through pressing Ctrl + Option + Shift + Down, and likewise Ctrl + Option + Shift + Up to exit that area when done
      • Press Enter or Ctrl + Option + Space to activate an item
    • NVDA on Windows
      • Use the arrow keys to move item by item
      • Press Enter to activate
  3. Take a look at the content with the screen reader:
    • Use Tab to navigate through the top links. What do you notice?
    • Turn on the screen reader and navigate to the images on the page. Anything that needs to be updated?
    • Can you navigate everywhere you need to? Is your focus position clear?
  4. Take a look at contrast ratios:
    • In addition to using the screen reader, take a look at the contrast on the page. Are the contrast ratios meeting at a minimum 4.5:1 for text to background?
    • Use the Lighthouse DevTools audit to see which areas are not meeting the minimum.
  5. Make a note of issues you come across, or places where you feel like the usability could be improved.

Scenarios:

  1. Use tab to navigate through the top headings. What do you notice?
  2. Turn on the screen reader and navigate to the images on the page. Anything that needs to be updated?
  3. Read the content on the page. Can you tell using a screen reader what’s the image that’s relevant to the page?
